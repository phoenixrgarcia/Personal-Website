About Me

I am a Software Engineering undergraduate with a minor in 'Software Development for the Mining Industry' seeking a summer internship for 2024. I have a particular interest in Data Engineering, Autonomous Systems, and Software Security. I'm looking to explore the software industry and see where my skills can be applied.


When I first became a student at the University of Arizona, I chose to study Mining Engineering with a minor in Softawre Engineering. I was enamored with the giant machines that are involved in the mining industry and I wanted to be involved in making and programming them. To me, the mining industry is always on the front line of innovation in automation and robotics. This was my plan until I had the opportunity to work as a mining engineering intern. Even though this position was a positive eperience, I came to realize that my plan of study would not let me achieve my goals of developing tools for the mining industry. As of Fall 2023, I'm happy to say I've found the Software Engineering degree to be a good fit for me.


I've spent my time at the Univerisity of Arizona working with Dr. Nathalie Risso in the Mine Automation and Autonomous Systems Laboratory. Here, I've worked to develop educational material for mining undergraduates on how to program drones for use in the mining industry. This coming semester I will be working to create a simulation of a mineral processing operation to give these undergraduates experience in making operational decisions in mineral processing and exposure to the uses of data infrastructure in the mining industry.

My summer as a metallurgical intern was split between my intern project and side projects. My main project was conducting a roaster oxygen flow trial. I’d sample ore, process the ore in the metallurgical lab, and analyze the results. Based on my data, I suggested reducing the oxygen flow to the roaster to increase gold recovery. Projects

GUI for Scholarship Management Service

My team was tasked with deriving the software requirements for and implementing a subsystem of a mock 'Univeristy of Arizona Scholarship Application Management System' (UASAMS). First, we captured the whole system in a series of diagrams and derived requirements for various subsystems of the UASAMS. We used Jama to keep track of our requirements through this process

My team members and I chose to develop the Front-End GUI for the UASAMS. We used tools for Java including JavaFX, Scene Builder, and GitHub to complete the project. I personally created the GUIs for four of our user types, with a focus on meeting the different requirements established in our elicitation. In writing this project I learned how to use anonymous inner classes, singletons, and lambda expressions.

'Good Cities' Relational Database

I'm unsure of what city I'd like to move to when I graduate, but I know that I want a walkable city thats colder and wetter than Tucson. This motivated me to create a relational database that would let me query different climate and walkability metrics to return different locations in the US. In a jupyter notebook I developed an ETL pipeline to extract data from the Smart Location Database and the NCEI Climate Database.

I explored this data, and cleaned out rows and columns that would not be in the scope of my RDB. Then, I transformed the data to fit together through zipcode databases from the United States Postal Service. Then, I uploaded this RDB to Amazon Web Services and ran some test queries.

This project taught me about using python's pandas for handling large amounts of data, how to work with AWS, and relational database design.
